## Summary

The author argues AI does not threaten higher education but exposes that we've been measuring proxies (outputs, coherence) rather than real learning (judgement, reasoning, epistemic rigour). The response should be to re-centre education on justification, verification, and awareness of uncertainty — not to ban AI or double down on surveillance.
