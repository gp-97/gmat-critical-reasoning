## Summary

The article argues that school WhatsApp groups, originally created for practical coordination, are now fueling a toxic culture of parental comparison and anxiety that harms children's confidence and curiosity. The author recommends mindful participation by parents and clear guidelines from schools rather than complete withdrawal from these groups.

## Key Analytical Takeaways

- **Correlation ≠ Causation:** The article observes behavior *in* WhatsApp groups and concludes the groups *cause* anxiety — confusing venue with cause.
- **Overgeneralization:** From anecdotal observation of "many such groups" the author generalizes to "millions of parents" across India.
- **Normative Leap:** The argument moves from describing behavior to prescribing a specific solution without justifying why this particular intervention is correct.
